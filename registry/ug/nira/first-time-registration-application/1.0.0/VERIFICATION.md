## Candidate selection

GOV-4353 (child of GOV-4351, "GovSchema Standard Research"), closing the last
item of Uganda's pre-scouted 3-of-6 backlog (see CATALOG.md's Known Gaps
entry 0e) after `ug/mowt/driving-licence-application` (GOV-4335/4337, DMV

## Reaching the live source

Target:
`https://www.nira.go.ug/media/2025/07/Form-3-NIRA-First-Time-Registration-Form.pdf`.

- The bare `nira.go.ug` host still times out on connect — reproduced this
  cycle, matching both prior cycles' own reports. The `www.` subdomain,
  however, resolves and serves the document over plain HTTP 200.
- The host also has an incomplete TLS certificate chain — a server
  misconfiguration, confirmed by `curl -k` (disabling strict certificate
  verification) succeeding where a default, strict-verification fetch fails.
  This is unrelated to the bare-host connect timeout; both had to be worked
  around independently.
- Independently re-fetched and re-hashed rather than trusted from the issue
  brief alone: HTTP 200, 312,831 bytes, sha256
  `37e7df2b2800bd6e2e210944cd02f1c08ed265c924b2775fedc1b70cb54e4623` —
  byte-for-byte and hash-for-hash match against the issue's own reported
  figures.
- Confirmed mechanically: the retrieved bytes begin `%PDF-1.7` and contain
  zero `/AcroForm`, `/Widget`, and `/FT` occurrences — a flat, print-and-fill
  specimen, not an interactive AcroForm PDF.
- Also confirmed `https://www.nira.go.ug/` itself resolves (HTTP 200, page
  title `NIRA | National Identification & Registration Authority`),
  supporting `authority.url`.

## Extraction method

Text extracted via `pdfjs-dist`'s `getTextContent()` API (vendored at
`/tmp/node_modules/pdfjs-dist`) across all 4 pages, walking each page's
text-run items by their exact x/y position (not rounded y-bucketing alone)
to reconstruct multi-column rows precisely — several rows on this form pack
2 or 3 field labels into one visual line (e.g. "Country" / "District" as a
2-column row; "Country" / "District" / "County" as a 3-column row elsewhere),
so precise x-ordering within a y-band was necessary to avoid misreading
column membership.

Page 4, the form's own "FORM FILLING INFORMATION GUIDE", was used as the
primary source for field descriptions and for the applicant-vs-"REGN
OFFICER" scoping calls, per the issue's own instruction, not just the
issue's own summary of it.

Two areas of ambiguous layout were additionally cross-checked by rendering
pages 1 and 2 to PNG via `pdfjs-dist` + `node-canvas` at 2.5x scale:

- The Part C.10 "Particulars of Applicant's Children" table, to count its
  literal printed row count (see Finding 7 below).
- The multi-column Country/District/County/Sub-County/Parish-Ward/Village
  address grids, to confirm the 2-column vs 3-column row layouts described
  in Finding 8 below.

Models 166 `fields[]` across 12 steps, all applicant-supplied (per the
page-4 guide's own "REGN OFFICER" attribution for every field this schema
excludes).

**Post-authoring correction (GOV-4351 duplicate-run reconciliation):** this
schema originally omitted `motherMaidenNames` — the source's own Part C.8
"Mother's details" block prints Surname, Given Names, Other Names,
**Maiden Names**, Previous Names as five distinct printed labels, matching
the same maiden-name convention already given to the applicant's own Part
A.1 block and to Spouse Details (Part B.6); Father's details prints no
equivalent line. A concurrent duplicate authoring run of this same issue
(PR #657) modelled this field independently; cross-checked directly against
the raw extracted page-2 text before adding it here (`fields[]` count
165→166, `mother_details` step's field order also corrected to match the
source's own printed order: Surname, Given Names, Other Names, Maiden
Names, Previous Names, National ID Number...).

## What's excluded, and why

- The **Registration Location Code** and **Application ID** at the top of
  Part A: per the page-4 guide, the location code is filled by the "REGN
  OFFICER", and the Application ID is "To be generated by the Application"
  (i.e. system-generated, not applicant-supplied).
- **Part D** in its entirety ("Form Section to be retained by the
  Registration Officer") — a condensed duplicate of Parts A-C, kept for the
  officer's own retained copy, not new applicant data.
- **Part F** in its entirety ("FOR OFFICIAL USE ONLY") — citizenship
  assessment, documentary-proof ticks, and the registration officer's own
  name/signature/date.

This mirrors this registry's established practice of excluding
officer/witness-only sections (e.g. `ug/mowt/driving-licence-application`'s
exclusion of the doctor's own Medical Examination Report).

## Disclosed source-fidelity findings

1. **No per-field required/optional markers exist anywhere in Parts A-C,
   except where the source itself prints an explicit textual condition**
   (e.g. "Fill if By registration, Naturalisation or Dual"). In that
   absence, this schema marks a field `required: true` only where it
   captures core identifying or administrative-boundary data indispensable
   to a national identity registration (name, sex, date of birth, and the
   Country/District/County/Sub-County/Parish-Ward/Village hierarchy for
   Place of Residence/Birth/Origin, plus `citizenshipType`), and
   `required: false` for auxiliary demographic, contact, or descriptive data
   (contacts, education, profession, occupation, religion, disabilities,
   other/previous/maiden names, street/plot-level address detail,
   passport/file numbers, and every Part C family-member field — see
   Finding 5). This is a disclosed judgment call, not a source-stated rule.

2. **`registrationType` ("FORM 3 CHILD ADULT (Tick Appropriate)") gates
   Part B in its entirety**, per the source's own Part B header, "(to be
   filled for Adult registration only)". Modelled as a required 2-value
   enum; every Part B field carries a matching `requiredWhen`/`visibleWhen`
   on `registrationType equals ADULT`.

3. **The five "Disabilities" checkboxes (Blind, Deaf, Physical, Mental,
   Dumb) are modelled as five independent optional booleans, not a
   single-select enum.** More than one can apply simultaneously (e.g. Deaf
   and Dumb, or Blind and Physical) and the source prints no
   mutual-exclusivity marker between them; v0.3's field-type vocabulary has
   no multi-select/array primitive (GSP-0009, not yet accepted), so this
   follows the independent-boolean-per-checkbox precedent this registry's
   own `my/jpj/driving-licence-application` (`physicalDisability`, etc.)
   established for a comparable non-exclusive checkbox row.

4. **The Spouse Details block (Part B.6) is modelled `requiredWhen`/
   `visibleWhen` an `all` of `registrationType equals ADULT` and
   `maritalStatus equals MARRIED`, though the source itself states no such
   condition explicitly** — Part B's own header conditions the whole Part
   on Adult registration, but nothing on the form ties Spouse Details
   specifically to a Married marital status; this is the disclosed judgment
   call the issue itself flagged, made because a spouse-details block is
   not logically answerable by a Single/Divorced/Widowed/Separated
   applicant. Within the block, `spouseSurname`/`spouseGivenNames`/
   `spouseCitizenshipType`/`placeOfMarriage`/`dateOfMarriage`/
   `typeOfMarriage`/`marriageCertificateNumber`/`otherSpouses` inherit this
   same conditional requiredness; `spouseOtherNames`/`spousePreviousNames`/
   `spouseMaidenName`/`spouseNationalIdNumber`/`spouseDualCitizenship`/
   `spouseCitizenshipUnderArticle9` remain unconditionally optional even
   within the block (per Finding 1's general policy).

5. **The Father's, Mother's, and Grandparent(s)/Introducer blocks (Part
   C.7-9) carry no outer `requiredWhen`/`visibleWhen` gate at all — every
   field in all three blocks is unconditionally optional.** Section 9's own
   printed condition — "if information of both parents is not available for
   Applicants under Citizenship by Birth/ descent" — establishes that
   father/mother information is not always available, which is why the
   Grandparent/Introducer fallback exists; by the same logic this schema
   does not fabricate a `required: true` on any father/mother field either.
   GSP-0013's Condition grammar also has no "field is absent" leaf operator,
   so a conditional link from `grandparentSurname` to "father and mother
   details are both blank" could not be expressed even if the source stated
   it more mechanically. This mirrors the treatment the issue itself asked
   for.

6. **The `citizenshipCertificateNumber`/`otherNationality`
   conditional-requiredness wording is taken verbatim from the page-4
   guide, not inferred**: "Certificate Number Fill if By registration,
   Naturalisation or Dual" and "Other Nationality If DUAL Citizen (Double
   Citizen) then fill Other Country". Applied identically to the
   applicant's own, Father's, and Mother's citizenship blocks, per the
   guide's own "CITIZENSHIP OF FATHER Citizenship Type Same as Citizenship
   of Applicant" (and the equivalent line for Mother). The Spouse Details
   block (Part B.6), however, prints no "Citizenship Certificate Number"
   line at all between its own article-9 tick and its "If Dual..." line —
   confirmed by re-extracting the exact y-ordered text run on page 1 (the
   applicant's, Father's, and Mother's blocks each print that line;
   Spouse's does not) — so `spouseCitizenshipCertificateNumber` is not
   modelled at all, an asymmetry disclosed here rather than silently
   "fixed" by adding a field the source does not print.

7. **Part C.10 "Particulars of Applicant's Children" is modelled as 4
   bounded slots (`child1`..`child4`), not an unbounded array, because the
   source itself prints exactly 4 blank data rows beneath the table's own
   column headers** — confirmed by rendering page 2 to a PNG at 2.5x scale
   and measuring the printed row-divider line pixel positions (6 horizontal
   rules bounding 1 header row + 4 data rows, each ~15pt tall, ending well
   above the page-4 footer). This is a genuine literal-printed-row-count
   anchor, the convention this registry's prior bounded-repeating-slot
   precedents required, rather than the looser 1-2-slot "replicate this
   section" scoping this registry used for `zm/pacra/company-incorporation`'s
   unbounded director rows; v0.3 has no array/repeating-element primitive
   (GSP-0009, not yet accepted) so a fifth child exceeds this form's own
   printed capacity, a limitation disclosed here rather than silently
   dropped.

8. **Father's and Mother's Place of Residence "Street"/"House No." fields
   are modelled matching the applicant's own Place of Residence structure
   (`residenceStreet` + `residencePlotHouseNumber`), per the page-4 guide's
   own "Guidance same as Place of Residence" instruction for both parents,
   even though the raw extracted label text for this specific row differs
   slightly between the three blocks** (applicant: "Street" / "Plot / House
   No."; Father's printed row: "Street" / "Street/House No."; Mother's
   printed row folds Village/Street/House No. into one wider 3-column row
   rather than 2). Treated as the same two fields in all three blocks per
   the guide's own explicit equivalence, not fabricated from an assumption
   of visual similarity alone.

## Conformance

5 valid mock scenarios:

- A first-time child registrant (`registrationType: CHILD`), Part A/C only,
  Part B entirely absent, declaration completed by a parent.
- A first-time adult, single, citizen-by-birth applicant with no children.
- An adult, married-by-registration applicant with a dual-citizenship
  declaration, a spouse (also dual), one child, and the applicant's own
  father and mother both also declaring dual citizenship by
  registration/naturalization — exercising every `requiredWhen` branch at
  once.
- An adult applicant relying on the Grandparent/Introducer fallback, with
  both the Father's and Mother's blocks left entirely blank.
- An adult applicant with all 4 bounded child slots populated.

Plus mutation-control fixtures: one missing-required-field control for each
of the 29 statically-required fields; one missing-conditionally-required
control for a representative subset of 13 `requiredWhen` fields spanning
the applicant's own, spouse's, father's, and mother's citizenship/marriage
conditional branches; an invalid-enum control (`sex`); and an
unknown-top-level-field control — 49 fixtures total, committed under
`conformance/ug/nira/first-time-registration-application/1.0.0/`.

An ephemeral, from-scratch conformance checker (deriving required,
`requiredWhen`, `enum`, and `integer` min/max rules directly from this
schema's own `fields[]`, discarded after use, not committed) ran all
fixtures: all 5 valid scenarios at 0 errors, all 44 mutation controls each
raising exactly 1 error.

Validated clean with `node tools/validate.mjs` and `node
tools/validate-ajv.mjs`, individually and as part of the full registry run
(602/602 documents passing both validators). `registry-index.json`
regenerated via `npm run build-index` in `tools/govschema-client/`.
